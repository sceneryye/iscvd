About Us

The International Society for Cardiovascular Diseases shall operate exclusively for educational and charitable purposes, including, but not limited to, reducing the incidence of cardiovascular diseases worldwide and improving the quality of life of those individuals affected by cardiovascular diseases by teaching healthcare professionals to better care for those individuals affected by cardiovascular diseases and by educating the public in methods to prevent and reduce cardiovascular diseases.

What is Cardiovascular Disease?

Heart and blood vessel disease — also called heart disease — includes numerous problems, many of which are related to a process called atherosclerosis. Atherosclerosis is a condition that develops when a substance called plaque builds up in the walls of the arteries. This buildup narrows the arteries, making it harder for blood to flow through. If a blood clot forms, it can stop the blood flow. This can cause a heart attack or stroke.
